# Google Play Store App Analytics

## Project Overview

A comprehensive data analysis of the Android app market by examining thousands of apps in the Google Play Store. This project explores app characteristics, ratings, categories, pricing strategies, and user reviews to uncover insights about the Android app ecosystem.

## Dataset

- **Source**: Scraped from Google Play Store by Lavanya Gupta (2018)
- **Original Dataset**: [Kaggle - Google Play Store Apps](https://www.kaggle.com/lava18/google-play-store-apps)
- **Contents**: App metadata including categories, ratings, reviews, size, installs, pricing, and more

## Key Questions Explored

- What are the characteristics of highly-rated apps?
- How do free apps compare to paid apps in terms of ratings and popularity?
- Which app categories are most popular and profitable?
- What is the relationship between app size, installs, and ratings?
- How do app prices correlate with ratings and user engagement?
- What patterns exist in user reviews and sentiment?

## Technologies Used

- **Python 3.13**
- **Pandas** - Data manipulation and analysis
- **NumPy** - Numerical computations
- **Matplotlib/Seaborn** - Data visualization

## Project Structure

The analysis includes:

1. **Data Loading & Exploration** - Understanding the dataset structure
2. **Data Cleaning** - Handling missing values, duplicates, and data type conversions
3. **Exploratory Data Analysis** - Statistical summaries and distributions
4. **Category Analysis** - Examining app categories and their characteristics
5. **Rating Analysis** - Understanding what drives high ratings
6. **Pricing Strategy Analysis** - Comparing free vs paid apps
7. **Review Analysis** - Sentiment and review patterns
